# Headlines, Hooks & Openers

## Headline formulas (Caples + 100 Ideas + USL)

| Formula | Structure | General example |
|---|---|---|
| How-to | "How to [achieve outcome]" | "How to build a weekly planning system that actually survives Monday" |
| Reason Why | "[Number] Reasons Why [Claim]" | "4 reasons your onboarding emails are not converting" |
| Question | "Do You [Experience]?" | "Do you know which task is slowing the whole team down?" |
| News | "New [finding/change] shows [claim]" | "New data shows customers abandon checkout earlier than teams think" |
| Command | "[Action] before [risk/problem]" | "Check this before you launch the campaign" |
| Curiosity | "What [X] doesn't tell you about [Y]" | "What your analytics dashboard doesn't tell you about churn" |
| Testimonial | "I tried [X] and [result]" | "I replaced daily standups with one dashboard and cut follow-ups in half" |
| Specific | "[Number]% of [audience] [action/problem]" | "62% of trial users drop before seeing the core feature" |

## Hook templates

**Story hook:**
> "I thought the problem was the campaign. It was the handoff after signup."

**Curiosity hook:**
> "One small step in the workflow is doing more damage than the obvious bottleneck."

**Question hook:**
> "When was the last time a customer did exactly what your landing page asked them to do?"

**Pattern-interrupt hook:**
> "The best-performing CTA may not be the loudest one on the page."

**Problem-first hook:**
> "The problem with most launch plans is not ambition. It's the missing owner for the first 48 hours."

**Aspirational hook:**
> "Imagine opening your dashboard and knowing exactly what needs attention first."

## Opening rules

1. **Start in the moment, not the reflection**
   - ❌ "In this post, we'll explore how to improve onboarding"
   - ✅ "A user signs up, clicks once, and disappears before seeing the product's best feature"

2. **No throat-clearing**
   - Cut: "Here's what to know before doing so"
   - Cut: "It's important to note that..."
   - Cut: "Let's take a closer look at..."

3. **Open with the reader's thought**
   - "You're probably wondering why more traffic still isn't producing more trials."
   - First sentence should match what is already running through their head.

4. **One hook per asset**
   - Don't stack hooks. Pick one emotional entry point and follow it.

5. **Specific wins**
   - "Your onboarding is unclear" → "The first email asks for three actions before explaining the payoff"
