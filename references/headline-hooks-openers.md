# Headlines, Hooks & Openers

## Headline Formulas (Caples + 100 Ideas + USL)

| Formula | Structure | BP Week 1 Example |
|---|---|---|
| How-to | "How to [achieve outcome]" | "How to check your BP without panic" |
| Reason Why | "[Number] Reasons Why [Claim]" | "4 Reasons Your BP Reading Might Be Wrong" |
| Question | "Do You [Experience]?" | "Do you trust that one BP number?" |
| News | "New Study Shows [Finding]" | "New study: 1 reading isn't enough" |
| Command | "[Action] and [Outcome]" | "Check this before you panic about BP" |
| Curiosity | "What [X] Doesn't Want You to Know" | "What your BP monitor isn't telling you" |
| Testimonial | "I tried [X] and [Result]" | "I almost stopped my BP meds over one number" |
| Specific | "[Number]% of People [Action]" | "68% of home BP readings need context" |

## Hook Templates (100 Ideas + ENC + EWS)

**Story hook:**
> "I almost stopped taking my BP medication because of one number. That mistake almost cost me."

**Curiosity hook:**
> "One BP reading can look dangerous because of one thing most people don't check."

**Question hook:**
> "When was the last time you checked your blood pressure properly?"

**Pattern-interrupt hook:**
> "Your blood pressure might be fine. The machine might be wrong."

**Problem-first hook:**
> "The problem with checking BP at home isn't the machine. It's the way most people use it."

**Aspirational hook:**
> "Imagine knowing your BP is under control without obsessing about the numbers."

## Opening Rules

1. **Start in the moment, not the reflection** (Collier)
   - ❌ "In this post, we'll explore how to check BP properly"
   - ✅ "You check your BP, the number looks high, and suddenly everything changes"

2. **No throat-clearing** (Humaniser #26)
   - Cut: "Here's what to know before doing so"
   - Cut: "It's important to note that..."
   - Cut: "Let's take a closer look at..."

3. **Open with the reader's thought** (RC: Bait Principle)
   - "You're probably wondering whether this number means something serious."
   - First sentence should match what's already running through their head.

4. **One hook per asset** (RA: USP)
   - Don't stack hooks. Pick one emotional entry point and follow it.

5. **Specific wins** (SA: Hopkins)
   - "Your arm was hanging wrong" → "Your arm was 4 inches below heart level"
