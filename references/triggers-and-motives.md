# Triggers & Motives

## Life-Force 8 (Cashvertising — Whitman)
Biologically hardwired desires. Use at least one per asset, but keep it ethical and proportional to the offer.

| # | Desire | General application |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Survival, life extension | "Stop small safety issues before they become expensive emergencies." |
| 2 | Enjoyment of food/drink | "Get the result without giving up the parts of your routine you enjoy." |
| 3 | Freedom from fear, pain, danger | "Know what to do next instead of guessing under pressure." |
| 4 | Sexual companionship | Use only where the product is genuinely relevant; protect privacy and dignity. |
| 5 | Comfortable living | "Skip the errand, queue, or repeated manual work." |
| 6 | Being superior, winning | "Use the system experienced operators use before the problem becomes visible." |
| 7 | Care and protection of loved ones | "Make the safer choice for the people depending on you." |
| 8 | Social approval | "Join teams/customers who already made this their default." |

## Six Prime Motives (Collier)
Blend 1-3 motives per piece. More than that usually makes the copy muddy.

| Motive | General application |
|---|---|
| **Love** | Protect someone, support a team, reduce someone else's burden. |
| **Gain** | Save money, time, effort, risk, or missed opportunity. |
| **Duty** | Do the responsible thing before neglect becomes costly. |
| **Pride** | Be the kind of person/team that handles this properly. |
| **Self-indulgence** | Make the process easier, smoother, more convenient. |
| **Self-preservation** | Avoid loss, failure, embarrassment, danger, or unnecessary stress. |

## Language-pattern stacking
Use language patterns to reinforce the motive already present in the copy. Do not add them as decoration.

| Pattern | Mechanism | General use |
|---|---|---|
| Toward/Away framing | Names both the desired outcome and avoided pain | "Get [outcome] without [old problem]." |
| Presupposition | Treats the next step as normal | "When you set up [action], [benefit] starts happening." |
| Future pacing | Lets the reader rehearse the outcome | "Imagine opening this next week and already knowing what to do." |
| Reframe | Changes the meaning of the objection | "The real cost isn't the tool. It's the repeated workaround." |

## Key triggers from Exactly What to Say (Jones)
Use as openers or transitions in copy.

| Phrase | Mechanism | General use |
|---|---|---|
| "Before you make your mind up..." | Lowers defences | Before addressing a likely objection. |
| "Just imagine..." | Mental rehearsal | Help the reader picture the after-state. |
| "What would that be worth to you?" | Value elicitation | Connect benefit to business/personal cost. |
| "Most people don't know that..." | Curiosity + insider | Introduce a non-obvious but useful fact. |
| "How would you feel if..." | Emotional rehearsal | Show relief, control, confidence, or safety. |
| "If I could show you [X], would you [Y]?" | Pre-close | Pair a proof promise with a low-friction next step. |
| "The good news is..." | Positive pivot | After naming a problem or risk. |
| "What happens next is..." | Process clarity | Explain onboarding, purchase, delivery, or follow-up. |
