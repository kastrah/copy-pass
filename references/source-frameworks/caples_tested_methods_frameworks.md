Framework summary from known industry knowledge about the book:

## Key Frameworks (known from copywriting literature)

### 1. Headline Formulas
- "How to" headlines — promise practical knowledge
- "Which" headlines — comparison/selection
- "Who" headlines — targeting
- "How I/We" headlines — personal story
- "If" headlines — conditional promise
- News headlines — announcement format
- Testimonial headlines — social proof
- Command headlines — direct instruction

### 2. 7-Step Copy Structure
1. Get attention (headline + visual)
2. Show a need or problem
3. Satisfy that need with your solution
4. Prove your product's merit
5. Persuade the reader to act
6. Ask for action (specific CTA)
7. Make response easy

### 3. Specific > General (confirmed by Hopkins)
"Never say 'economical' — say 'saves you 23%'"

### 4. Testing Principle
Test one variable at a time. Small sample first, then scale winners.

### 5. Direct Response vs General
DR = measurable action. Every piece must track.

### 6. Small Space Rules
- One idea per ad
- Big promise in headline
- Simple layout
- Coupon or response mechanism

### 7. Price Presentation
- Anchor high
- Break down to daily/weekly cost
- Compare to value received

### 8. Copy Ladder
Headline → Subhead → Opening → Body → Close → PS → CTA

## Known formulas from Caples' ads:
- "They laughed when I sat down at the piano..."
- "Do you make these mistakes in English?"
- Pattern: Identify → Agitate → Solve
