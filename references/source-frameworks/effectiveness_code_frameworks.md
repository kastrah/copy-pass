# The Effectiveness Code — Copywriting & Persuasion Framework Notes

**Source:** James Hurman with Peter Field, Cannes Lions/WARC effectiveness study.

## Core usable idea
Creative work becomes commercially powerful when it is tied to a clear effectiveness objective, given enough commitment, and measured by outcomes beyond short-term engagement. Do not confuse attention metrics with business effects. Buzz is useful when it becomes a stepping stone to brand, behavior, sales, or profit outcomes.

## The Creative Effectiveness Ladder
Use the ladder to decide what the copy must accomplish before writing.

1. **Influential Idea**
   - Goal: attention, sharing, earned reach, campaign recall.
   - Copy job: make the idea worth talking about.
   - Best for: launch hooks, PR-able angles, social-first campaigns, top-of-funnel awareness.
   - Watch-out: buzz alone is rarely the final objective.

2. **Behavioral Breakthrough**
   - Goal: get people to do something new or overcome inertia.
   - Copy job: reduce friction, dramatize the action, normalize participation.
   - Best for: habit shifts, signups, trials, switches, referrals, category education.

3. **Sales Spike**
   - Goal: short-term sales lift.
   - Copy job: clear offer, urgent reason to act, low ambiguity CTA.
   - Best for: promotions, launches, seasonal pushes, direct response.
   - Measure in: short windows, often up to three months.

4. **Brand Builder**
   - Goal: sustained improvements in awareness, salience, preference, perceived value.
   - Copy job: build memory structures, repeat distinctive ideas, attach emotion to the brand.
   - Best for: ongoing campaigns, category positioning, trust building.

5. **Commercial Triumph**
   - Goal: long-term business growth: sales, share, profit, penetration.
   - Copy job: connect brand meaning to commercial choice repeatedly over time.
   - Best for: major campaign platforms with enough duration and media support.

6. **Enduring Icon**
   - Goal: a campaign asset or platform that compounds for years.
   - Copy job: create repeatable language, characters, taglines, rituals, and brand codes that can refresh without being replaced.
   - Best for: flagship brand platforms.

## Objective-setting checklist
Before writing, define:
- **Primary ladder level:** Are we seeking buzz, behavior, sales, brand growth, commercial growth, or enduring fame?
- **Measurement window:** Three months for spikes; 12+ months for commercial growth; multi-year for iconic platforms.
- **Behavior required:** What must the audience think, feel, remember, say, or do?
- **Proof of effectiveness:** Which metric would prove the copy worked?
- **Role of attention:** Is engagement the objective, or is it fuel for a deeper outcome?

## Creative Commitment framework
Effectiveness rises when a strong idea receives more commitment across three levers:

- **Spend:** enough reach/frequency to be noticed.
- **Duration:** enough time for memory and behavior to build.
- **Channels:** enough touchpoints for reinforcement.

**Copy application:** do not write one-off messages when the objective requires compounding. Build modular copy systems:
- Master claim or platform idea.
- Repeated tagline or phrase.
- Channel-specific executions.
- Short activation copy for conversion moments.
- Long-form or educational copy for belief-building.

## “Land and expand” principle
If a message is working, extend it instead of replacing it too quickly.

Checklist:
- Can the same core idea run longer?
- Can it be adapted to more formats?
- Can the proof be strengthened?
- Can the brand asset be made more distinctive?
- Can high-performing language become a repeatable tagline, email subject pattern, ad hook, or sales script?

## Influential Idea patterns
To create talkability, look for concepts that violate expectations without violating trust.

Reusable angles:
- **Act against type:** do what people do not expect from a brand, category, or authority.
- **Enter a cultural moment:** connect to a conversation already in motion, but make the brand role relevant.
- **Create public jeopardy:** make the idea feel like something is at stake.
- **Make the audience a participant:** give people a behavior, phrase, challenge, or symbol to spread.
- **Use originality plus clarity:** surprising ideas must still be instantly understood.

Copy prompts:
- “What would this category never normally admit?”
- “What convention can we break in a useful way?”
- “What would make someone quote this to a friend?”
- “What is the headline journalists or social users would repeat?”

## Brand-building copy principles
- Prioritize **emotion, memory, and distinctiveness** over pure information when the goal is long-term growth.
- Repeat brand codes: names, colors, phrases, characters, symbols, rituals, sonic cues, visual structures.
- Build associations around the buying situation: when the need appears, the brand should come to mind.
- Avoid excessive novelty. Refresh executions, not the strategic platform.

## Activation copy principles
For sales spikes, make action easy:
- State the offer plainly.
- Make the benefit immediate.
- Add a real reason to act now.
- Remove uncertainty with specifics.
- Use direct CTAs.
- Measure quickly and iterate.

## Effectiveness planning formula
**Objective + Audience response + Creative idea + Commitment + Measurement = Effective campaign plan**

Use it as a brief:
- We need to achieve: `[ladder level/outcome]`
- By getting the audience to: `[think/feel/do]`
- Through a creative idea that: `[surprise/emotion/distinctive asset]`
- Supported by: `[duration/channels/budget]`
- Measured by: `[metric and time window]`

## Copywriter’s diagnostic
A draft is weak if:
- It optimizes for likes but has no business outcome.
- It changes language or positioning every execution.
- It has a CTA but no brand memory.
- It has brand emotion but no eventual path to action.
- It asks for long-term results from a short-term burst.
- It relies on media spend to compensate for an uninteresting idea.

## Practical application notes
- Write campaigns as systems, not isolated assets.
- Separate brand-building copy from activation copy, then make sure they reinforce each other.
- Use engagement as distribution fuel, not proof of success by itself.
- Give strong ideas enough time and repetition to compound.
- When constrained by budget, increase duration, channel reuse, and distinctiveness where possible.
