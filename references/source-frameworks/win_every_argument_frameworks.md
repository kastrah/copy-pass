# How to Win Every Argument — Framework Summary

**Book by Madsen Pirie (2006)**

Encyclopedia of logical fallacies. Useful for pre-empting objections and making airtight arguments in copy.

## Key Fallacies/Frameworks Most Applicable to Copywriting

### 1. Straw Man — Frame Competitors Weakly
Don't attack competitors directly. Restate their position in its weakest form.

### 2. Appeal to Authority — Use Expert Credibility
Cite medical authorities, regulatory, PCN. Transfer their credibility.

### 3. The Slippery Slope — Show Consequences
"If you delay treatment, the next step could be..." (used carefully, ethically in healthcare)

### 4. Appeal to Fear — Motivate Through Caution
"Fake drugs kill 100,000+ Africans each year." Follow with solution.

### 5. The False Dilemma — Narrow Choices
"Either you order from us now, or you risk buying counterfeit medicine." 

### 6. Begging the Question — Assert the Conclusion
Do not prove what still needs proving. Instead of vague status claims, show concrete proof.

### 7. Hasty Generalization — Use anecdotally
One strong testimonial = "all our customers feel this way."

### 8. Post Hoc — Implied Causation
"Since switching to the new process, response time fell by 30%." (True: better follow-through)

## Ethical Application for Healthcare
Most fallacies are unethical if used to deceive. But for healthcare:
- Use appeal to authority (doctors endorse = legitimate)
- Use slippery slope carefully only when the consequences are real and proportionate
- Use fear + solution (danger + specific action = ethical persuasion)
