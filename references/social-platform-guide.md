# Social Platform Guide

## X / Twitter
- Lead with the sharpest idea, not context.
- One thought per post.
- Threads need progression: claim → proof → implication → action.
- Avoid generic engagement bait.

## LinkedIn
- Lead with a work moment, decision, lesson, or useful tension.
- Keep paragraphs short.
- Give readers a practical takeaway they can use without buying anything.
- Avoid sounding like a keynote speaker trapped in a caption.

## Instagram carousel
- Slide 1: clear promise or tension.
- Slides 2-6: one idea per slide.
- Final slide: one action.
- Design copy must be shorter than caption copy.

## Reels / short video
- First 2 seconds must name the situation or tension.
- Use spoken language, not article language.
- Show one useful idea; do not compress a full essay into 30 seconds.
- End with one clear next step.

## Email / newsletter
- Subject line sells the open.
- First line confirms the email is worth reading.
- Body should explain the mechanism and make the next step obvious.
- CTA should match the reader's belief level.

## Landing page
- Hero: promise + audience + mechanism.
- Body: proof, objections, process, and outcomes.
- CTA: repeated consistently, not reinvented in every section.
- Remove any section that does not increase belief or action.

## Cross-platform rule
One core promise per campaign. Change the format, proof, and depth by platform; do not change the message every time.
